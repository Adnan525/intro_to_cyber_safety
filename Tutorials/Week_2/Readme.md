## 1. What is Cyber Safety and Why Does It Matter?
### Discussion Question
*What is cyber safety, and why is it more important now than ever before?*

### Key Concepts

**Cyber safety** is the process of using the services and resources of the Internet in a safe and responsible manner. It encompasses not only the protection of your data, but also your personal safety and the safety of those around you. The goal of cyber safety is to safeguard your computer, personal information, and loved ones from attacks and other potential threats — both malicious and accidental.

**Why is it increasingly important?**
The safety concerns that exist in the physical world have always required awareness and caution. However, the virtual world introduces a new and expanding set of risks that compound those concerns. As more aspects of daily life — banking, communication, healthcare, education — move online, the potential impact of threats grows significantly.

## 2. Cyber Safety vs Cyber Security

### Discussion Question
*What is the difference between cyber safety and cyber security?*

### Key Concepts

Although the terms are often used interchangeably, they refer to distinct areas of concern:

| Term | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Cyber Security** | The protection of networks, computers, and the infrastructure over which the Internet is delivered. It is primarily concerned with protecting data and information systems from unauthorised access, damage, or attack. |
| **Cyber Safety** | The protection of users — particularly individuals — from harmful online content, inappropriate interactions, and other dangers encountered in the digital environment. |

In short, cyber security protects the *systems*, while cyber safety protects the *people* who use them.

## 3. Social Media: Data Collection and Content Ownership

### Discussion Question
*Investigate your favourite social media platform. What kind of information does it collect from users, and who owns the user-generated content?*

### Key Concepts

**Terms of Service and Content Ownership**

Whenever you sign up for an online account or use a website, you agree to its Terms of Service. It is important to read these agreements carefully, as they define who owns the content you create.

Ownership arrangements vary considerably across platforms. For example:

- **Facebook** states that users retain ownership of the content they post, though they grant Facebook a licence to use it.
- **Amazon** allows users to retain ownership of reviews they submit, even when those reviews appear on third-party sites.
- **The New York Times** takes a notably different approach, with its Terms of Service granting the publication a perpetual, non-exclusive, worldwide, royalty-free, sub-licensable licence over submitted content — and users effectively waive the right to object to how the material is altered or used.

**User-Generated Content vs User-Generated Data**

It is important to distinguish between these two concepts:

- **User-generated content** refers to material that a user actively creates and publishes — such as a photo, review, or post. Copyright over such content can typically be defined and transferred through licensing agreements within a platform.

- **User-generated data** refers to information produced *about* a user as a result of their activity online — including browser type, public IP address, time logs, and cookies. This data is not authored by the user in any meaningful sense, and users do not hold copyright over it.

Privacy-conscious users can limit the sharing of some browsing data with their Internet Service Provider through the use of tools such as Virtual Private Networks (VPNs). However, it is worth noting that this does not confer ownership over that data.

## 4. Privacy Issues on Social Media

### Discussion Question
*When using social media, what are the key privacy issues users should be aware of?*

### Key Concepts

**Privacy Settings**

Most social media platforms provide privacy settings that allow users to control who can see their content and personal information. However, default settings are often permissive, and users may not be aware of what they have consented to share. Regularly reviewing and updating privacy settings is an important habit.

**Location and Geo-Tagging**

Many platforms automatically embed location data into photos and posts (known as geo-tagging). This can inadvertently reveal a user's home address, workplace, or daily routine to a wider audience than intended.

**The Cambridge Analytica Scandal**

A significant case study in social media privacy is the Cambridge Analytica scandal, in which data from millions of Facebook users was harvested — largely without explicit consent — and used to build psychological profiles for targeted political advertising. This case highlighted the risks associated with third-party app permissions and the broad reach of social media data policies. Read The Guardian article [here](https://www.theguardian.com/news/2018/mar/17/cambridge-analytica-facebook-influence-us-election).

## 5. Encryption: What It Is and Why It Matters

### Discussion Question
*What is encryption, and why is it important?*

### Key Concepts

**Encryption** is the process of encoding data so that only those who are authorised — and possess the correct key or password — can read it. Encrypted data is rendered unreadable to anyone who gains unauthorised access.

One of the most common everyday risks is physical theft or loss of a device. Without encryption, a thief can simply plug a storage device into another computer and access all of its contents. Encryption eliminates this vulnerability by making the data useless without the correct credentials.

### Task W01T02 — Encrypting a File or Folder in Windows 10

**Objective:** Encrypt a folder using Windows built-in encryption.

**Before you begin:** This task requires **Windows 10 Pro** (not the Home edition). If you are using a different version of Windows, you may use free third-party tools such as [VeraCrypt](https://veracrypt.codeplex.com) or [CipherShed](https://ciphershed.org/).

Create a new folder and rename it `IntroCyber_YourUCID` (replacing `YourUCID` with your actual university ID). Copy the file you created in Task W01T01 into this folder.

**Steps:**

1. Right-click on the `IntroCyber_YourUCID` folder and select **Properties**.
2. Click **Advanced**.
3. Tick the box labelled **Encrypt contents to secure data**.
4. Click **OK**, then **Apply**.
5. If prompted, select whether to encrypt the folder only or its contents as well. Choose the option appropriate for your situation, then click **OK**.

> **Note:** Windows offers several encryption methods, including BitLocker (for full-disk encryption on Pro editions) and file/folder-level encryption as used in this task. Both are valid approaches depending on your needs.
